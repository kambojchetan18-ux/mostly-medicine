# Social drafts — IELTS vs OET for AHPRA in 2026: Which English Test Should an IMG Doctor Take?

## LinkedIn (Amandeep)

If you've ever stared at "IELTS vs OET" in a search bar at 11pm, wondering which test AHPRA actually prefers — I have news: they don't prefer either one.

I sat OET Medicine in Delhi after weighing both seriously. Here's what I learned:

**AHPRA accepts four English tests** — IELTS Academic, OET Medicine, PTE Academic, TOEFL iBT — with zero hierarchy between them. Your registration application is treated identically whichever you choose.

**The real difference is content, not difficulty.** OET is built around clinical scenarios (patient letters, doctor-patient role-plays). IELTS uses general academic content (essays, lectures, broad passages). If you write referral letters every week and haven't written an essay in five years, OET's content matches your daily English use. That's the edge — not that it's "easier", but that it's closer to how you actually work.

**Cost matters, but not as much as fit.** IELTS Academic runs ~A$420; OET Medicine ~A$587. But OET now offers single-skill retakes — if you miss only Writing, you re-sit Writing alone, not the full test. That closes the gap over a full prep cycle.

**The honest truth:** Plan for one re-sit regardless. The most commonly missed component on both tests for IMGs is Writing — IELTS Task 2 essays or OET referral letters. Pick by your daily English habits, not by price.
