## LinkedIn (Amandeep)

Four years. That's the realistic timeline from your first day as an RMO in Australia to holding a permanent residency visa as an IMG doctor.

I say "realistic" because the visa stack — 482 Skills in Demand, then 186 Employer Nomination — isn't a sprint. It's a specific sequence tied to your starting point (Australian grad vs overseas-trained), your employer's sponsorship capacity, and whether you land in a standard city or a DAMA region (a designated area with migration concessions).

The messy truth: there's no single "best visa for doctors in Australia." There are roughly four pathways, each with different timelines and trade-offs. Australian graduates take the 485 → 482 → 186 route (around 5 years). Overseas IMGs go direct 482 → 186 (4–6 years). Regional DAMA agreements can shorten this if you're willing to work in designated areas.
