# Social drafts — How IMGs Land Their First RMO Job in Australia: State-by-State Strategy for 2026

## LinkedIn (Amandeep)

If you've just passed AMC Part 2 and you're thinking "now what?" — welcome to the part no exam prepared you for.

Australian RMO recruitment doesn't work like most countries IMGs come from. There's no national match, no central register, and every state runs its own cycle on its own calendar. I learned this the hard way while completing my recency of practice in India before moving back to Sydney.

Here's what actually matters:

**State cycles overlap but don't sync.** NSW opens in May (offers August-September), Victoria runs a computer match in June, Queensland uses a single-employer model. The mistake most IMGs make: apply to one state and wait. The right move: parallel applications across at least three states.

**DPA (Distribution Priority Area) decides where you can actually work.** If you're on a 482 visa, you can only access Medicare in DPA-classified locations — which means regional Australia and specific outer-metro pockets. Inner Sydney, inner Melbourne, inner Brisbane are out. Check your hospital's postcode at doctorconnect.gov.au before you apply.

**Your CV format matters more than you think.** Australian hospital CVs are 2-3 pages, reverse-chronological, with named referees and phone numbers. A 10-page CV with a photo gets deleted before anyone reads it.

Realistic timeline from arrival to first RMO contract (if your AMC, AHPRA and English are done): 3-6 months.
