# Social drafts — AMC Pass Rates by Country (2024-2026): What the Data Actually Shows IMGs

## LinkedIn (Amandeep)

I just finished my AMC Part 1 and Part 2, and I've spent the last few months watching my cohort obsess over one question: "What's the actual pass rate for doctors from my country?"

Honest answer? The AMC doesn't publish it.

Most of the "AMC pass rate India is X%" claims floating around forums are extrapolations from workforce data, not direct AMC stats. The AMC publishes aggregate numbers (around 60-70% first-attempt for Part 1), but no country-level breakdown. The GMC does this for PLAB; the AMC doesn't.

What actually matters more:

• **Years since graduation** — rust shows up fast on a broad-spectrum recall exam. Five years out without active practice? You'll feel it.

• **Timed MCQ volume** — this is the single strongest predictor of first-attempt success. Not hours studied. Not which textbook. Total practice MCQs under exam conditions.

• **Australian clinical context** — PBS drug names, eTG guidelines, Australian-style stems. General English fluency ≠ medical English fluency.

• **The second-attempt gap is massive** — repeat candidates pass at 15-20 percentage points higher rates, mostly because they've now seen the real exam interface and shifted to timed practice.
