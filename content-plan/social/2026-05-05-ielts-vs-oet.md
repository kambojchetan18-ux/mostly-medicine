# Social drafts — IELTS vs OET for AHPRA in 2026: Which English Test Should an IMG Doctor Take?

## LinkedIn (Amandeep)

If you've ever stared at "IELTS vs OET" in a search bar at midnight, wondering which test AHPRA actually prefers — I have news that might surprise you.

AHPRA doesn't prefer one. The Medical Board accepts four English tests for registration, and your application is treated identically whichever you choose. The real question is which test fits *your* life right now.

I sat OET Medicine in Delhi after finishing my AMC exams, and here's what actually matters:

**Content, not cost.** OET is built around clinical scenarios — patient letters, doctor-patient role-plays. IELTS is general academic essays and bar charts. If you write referral letters every week and haven't written an essay in five years, OET's closer to your daily English. But if you've been out of clinical work for a year, IELTS listening might actually feel easier.

**The bottleneck is Writing.** On both tests. IELTS: the essay. OET: the referral letter format. Plan for one re-sit — most IMGs need it, and OET now lets you re-sit a single failed sub-test instead of the whole exam.

**The numbers:** IELTS ~A$420, OET ~A$587. Both require 7.0 overall / Grade B in each sub-test respectively. Both are valid for 2 years.

The honest answer: pick by your daily English habits, not the price gap. I chose OET because I was working clinically. Your answer might be different.
