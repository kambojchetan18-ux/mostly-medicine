### Slide 5 · insight

**The real drivers: not nationality**

Years since graduation, medical English fluency, clinical practice depth, and structured timed-MCQ prep. In that order.

_Source: McGrath et al., MJA; Mostly Medicine internal data (n=136)_

🎨 **Visual:** Four stacked boxes, each labelled with one driver. Emphasis on 'timed MCQ prep' box (slightly larger, bolder).

### Slide 6 · stat

**Second-attempt pass rate: +15-20 points**

Repeat candidates pass at materially higher rates. Each attempt costs A$2,790. Plan before you sit.

_Source: amc.org.au/assessment/fees_

🎨 **Visual:** Arrow pointing upward. Two percentage figures: 65% (first attempt) → 80-85% (second attempt). Tabular-nums.

### Slide 7 · insight

**The strongest single predictor**

Total timed-condition MCQs completed. Users who completed 3000+ had meaningfully higher first-attempt pass rates.

_Source: Mostly Medicine internal data (n=136 active users)_

🎨 **Visual:** Upward curve graph. X-axis: MCQ volume (0 → 3000+). Y-axis: pass rate. Curve flattens after ~2500.
